About Umuganda

Umuganda is a traditional community work practice in Rwanda where citizens come together to contribute to the development of their communities. This monthly activity helps maintain public infrastructure, protect the environment, and strengthen social cohesion.

Participation in Umuganda is mandatory for all able-bodied citizens aged 18 to 65. The program has played a significant role in Rwanda's post-genocide reconstruction and continues to be a cornerstone of community development.

Through this portal, community administrators can manage Umuganda sessions, track participation, and ensure the smooth operation of this important national program.
